Understanding BMR and Daily Caloric Needs

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) represents the minimum number of calories your body burns to maintain basic life functions at rest. This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is considered one of the most accurate formulas for estimating BMR.

BMR Formula (Mifflin-St Jeor)

For Men:
BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) + 5

For Women:
BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) - 161

Activity Level Multipliers

  • Sedentary: × 1.2 (Office job, little exercise)
  • Light Activity: × 1.375 (Light exercise 1-3 days/week)
  • Moderate Activity: × 1.55 (Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week)
  • Very Active: × 1.725 (Hard exercise 6-7 days/week)
  • Extra Active: × 1.9 (Very hard exercise & physical job)

Weight Management Goals

  • Weight Loss: TDEE - 500 calories/day
  • Maintenance: TDEE
  • Weight Gain: TDEE + 500 calories/day

Note: A 500-calorie deficit or surplus typically results in about 1 pound (0.45 kg) of weight change per week.
