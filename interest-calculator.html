Understanding Interest Calculations

Interest is the cost of borrowing money or the return on investment. Understanding how it's calculated is crucial for making informed financial decisions.

Compound Interest Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal amount
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Time in years

Simple Interest Formula

A = P(1 + rt)

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal amount
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • t = Time in years

Common Compound Frequencies

  • Annually: 1 time per year
  • Semi-annually: 2 times per year
  • Quarterly: 4 times per year
  • Monthly: 12 times per year
  • Daily: 365 times per year
