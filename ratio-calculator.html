Explanation:

Understanding Ratios and Proportions

A ratio compares two quantities. It shows how much of one thing there is compared to another. For example, if there are 3 apples and 2 oranges, the ratio of apples to oranges is 3:2.

A proportion is an equation stating that two ratios are equal. For example, 1:2 = 3:6 is a proportion. This calculator can solve for a missing value in a proportion (like finding 'x' in A:B = C:x) or simplify a single ratio (like simplifying 6:12 to 1:2).

Simplifying Ratios

To simplify a ratio (e.g., A:B), find the greatest common divisor (GCD) of A and B. Divide both A and B by their GCD to get the simplest form.

Solving Proportions

In a proportion A:B = C:D, the product of the means (B and C) equals the product of the extremes (A and D). Use this cross-multiplication property (A × D = B × C) to solve for the unknown value.

Real-World Uses

Ratios are used in cooking (scaling recipes), maps (scale ratio), finance (debt-to-income ratio), and comparing quantities in various fields like science and engineering.
